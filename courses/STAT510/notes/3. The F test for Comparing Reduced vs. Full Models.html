3. The F test for Comparing Reduced vs. Full Models

  • Assume GMMNE and suppose C(X0)⊂C(X). We wish to test H0:E(y)∈C(X0) vs. HA:E(y)∈C(X)∖C(X0)

    • For the general case, consider the test statistics
    F=y′(PX−PX0)y/[rank(X)−rank(X0)]y′(I−PX)y/[n−rank(X)]∼Frank(X)−rank(X0),n−rank(X)(βX′(PX−PX0)Xβ2σ2)

    • Because (PX−PX0σ2)(σ2I)=PX−PX is idempotent and rank(PX−PX0)=rank(PX)−rank(PX0),

      y′(PX−PX0σ2)y∼χrank(X)−rank(X0)2(12β′X′(PX−PX0σ2)Xβ)

    • y′(I−PXσ2)y∼χn−rank(X)2

    • y′(PX−PX0σ2)y is independent of y′(I−PXσ2)y because (PX−PX0σ2)(σ2I)(I−PXσ2)=0

    • If H0 is true, then (PX−PX0)Xβ=0

    • β′X(PX−PX0)Xβ=∥(PX−PX0)Xβ∥2=∥Xβ−PX0Xβ∥2=∥E(y)−PX0E(y)∥2

    • y′(PX−PX0)y=y′(I−PX0)y−y′(I−PX)y=SSEREDUCED−SSEFULL

    • Thus, F=(SSEREDUCED−SSEFULL)/(DFEREDUCED−DEFFULL)SSEFULL/DFEFULL

    • Equivalence of F test: this reduced vs. full model F test is equivalent to the F test for testing H0:Cβ=d vs. HA:Cβ≠d.
